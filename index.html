In a grid emergency, the sequence of actions matters as much as the actions themselves. EHV Restoration Copilot follows a structured 6-step restoration framework aligned with CEA Grid Code and MSETCL/PGCIL SOPs — so you never miss a critical step under pressure.

RESTORATION SEQUENCE — EHV SUBSTATION
STEP 01
Fault Detection & Classification CRITICAL
Identify the fault type — earth fault, phase-to-phase, three-phase, or equipment failure. Analyse relay targets, event logs, SCADA alarms, and fault recorder data. Classify: transient, permanent, or equipment damage.
STEP 02
Impact Analysis CRITICAL
Determine scope — which feeders, transformers, and bus sections are affected. Identify load lost and priority loads (hospitals, railways, defence). Check inter-substation coupling and transfer capability.
STEP 03
Isolation Strategy OPERATIONAL
Isolate the faulted element using correct switching sequence. Verify isolation via protection indications, SF6 pressure, and disconnect visual confirmation. Issue PTW (Permit to Work) per safety protocol.
STEP 04
Restoration Plan OPERATIONAL
Identify alternate supply paths. Plan switching sequence to restore priority loads first. Check protection coordination for alternate configuration. Coordinate with SLDC/RLDC as required by Grid Code.
STEP 05
Safe Energisation OPERATIONAL
Step-by-step energisation of restored elements. Verify voltage, current, and power flow at each step. Monitor protection relays for any indication of remaining fault. Restore load in priority order.
STEP 06
System Stabilisation & Documentation COMPLETE
Confirm stable voltages, frequency, and power flow across the restored network. Document the event — relay operations, switching log, fault analysis, and corrective actions. Submit event report per CEA requirements.
